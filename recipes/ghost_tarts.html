Ghost Tarts

Description

A sweet treat with a taste that will follow you to the grave. A wonderful recipe to make as a witching hour snack or for a quick breakfast.

Ingredients

For the Tarts:

  • 2 cups all purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup salted butter
  • 3-4 tbsp ice water
  • 1/4 cup nutella (or another filling)

For the Glaze:

  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 5 tbsp milk
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Black nonpareils

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, stir the flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. Add cubed butter and beat until the mixture resembles course sand.
  3. Add the ice water 1 tbsp at a time until the dough comes together (you may not need all 4 tbsp).
  4. Divide your dough in 2 and flatten into discs.
  5. Wrap dough in plastic wrap and chill for 45 minutes to 1 hour.
  6. Roll out first ball of dough onto a light floured surface.
  7. Using a large cookie cutter, cut out 8 more ghosts.
  8. Spoon a couple tsp of filling and spread till about 1/3" is left around the edge.
  9. Roll out remaining dough and cut out 8 more ghosts.
  10. Wet the edge of the filled side with water and cover with other side; fork to seal.
  11. Poke air holes into the top of tart with fork.
  12. Bake at 350 degrees F for 30 minutes, or until golden brown.
  13. In a small sauceplan, combine powdered sugar, water, and vanilla extract.
  14. Cook over low-medium heat, whisking occasionally, until the glaze is warm and starts to form a crust on top. Remove from heat.
  15. Dip each tart into the glaze, gently shaking it to allow any excess glaze to drip off.
  16. Stir the glaze inbetween each tart to avoid a crust.
  17. Immediately add two black nonpareils to each tart for eyes.
